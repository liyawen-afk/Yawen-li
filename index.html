About Me

I am a Professor of Social Work at California State University, San Bernardino, where I also serve as Vice Chair of the Institutional Review Board and Executive Board Member of the Center on Aging.

My research focuses on long-term care services for older adults, with particular emphasis on adult day services, organizational capacity in healthcare settings, and health disparities among minority populations. I am especially interested in how community-based care organizations adapt to changing policy, demographic, and technological environments.

I am currently Principal Investigator on an NIH-funded study examining dynamic capabilities in adult day health services across the United States.

Education

  • MSW, University of Hong Kong, 2002
  • BA, Yunnan University, 1999

Research Interests

  • Adult day services and community-based long-term care
  • Organizational adaptation and dynamic capabilities in healthcare
  • Electronic health record adoption in long-term care settings
  • Health disparities among older Chinese Americans
  • Dementia care and caregiver support
